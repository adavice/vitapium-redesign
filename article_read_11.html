LEMON ZEST: BENEFITS FOR THE BODY

In addition to the juicy pulp of lemon, the brightly colored dense peel, called the zest, has become widely used in cooking and has tremendous health benefits.

Chemical composition

The zest is saturated with essential oils that keep well during drying. It also contains limonene, pellandren, hesperidin, salvestrol, citric, malic and formic acids, flavonoids, phytoncides, pectin, coumarins, vitamins (especially a lot of ascorbic acid), fiber, macro- and microelements. In terms of the content of vitamins, the peel is 2 times higher than the inner content of citrus - lemon juice.

Why is lemon peel good for you?

There is no question whether lemon peel is good for you. Due to its rich composition, it has a beneficial effect on the body:

  • - strengthens the immune system and enhances the body's ability to resist infections;
  • - increases the strength of bones, prevents premature wear of the joints and the development of arthritis;
  • - destroys fungal microflora;
  • - relieves sore throat;
  • - calms the nerves;
  • - eliminates vitamin deficiency;
  • - improves mood, relieves anxiety and depression;
  • - has a rejuvenating effect;
  • - serves as a natural antiseptic.

With the simultaneous use of zest with honey, chocolate and ginger, its effect is enhanced. In this case, it turns into an aphrodisiac and increases sexual desire.

When using the zest, it will bring special benefits to the cardiovascular system:

  • - activates cardiac activity;
  • - improves the elasticity of blood vessels;
  • - restores blood flow;
  • - stimulates the dissolution of blood clots;
  • - lowers blood pressure;
  • - prevents the development of thrombosis and varicose veins.

Due to its unique properties, lemon peel is beneficial for stomach disorders:

  • - activates intestinal motility;
  • - relieves spasms;
  • - relieves colic, vomiting, flatulence and dyspepsia;
  • - destroys putrefactive bacteria in the intestines and in the oral cavity, as a result, the unpleasant odor disappears and digestion improves;
  • - increases appetite;
  • - stops bleeding gums;
  • - treats gingivitis, scurvy;
  • - prevents the development of caries, periodontitis and periodontitis.

The substance limonene, which is part of the peel, is characterized by anti-tumor properties. Studies have shown that people who eat lemons with the skin are much less likely to develop malignant tumors in the colon, intestines, lungs and breasts. Also, the zest inhibits the oncology of the prostate and skin, blocks the spread of cancer cells and prevents metastasis.

Indications for use

Vitamin-rich lemon peel will benefit those who are losing weight. It speeds up metabolism, slows down the absorption of carbohydrates, eliminates cravings for sweets, and ensures long-term satiety.

The zest neutralizes free radicals, as a result of which it prevents premature aging, oncology, and cardiovascular pathologies. Due to the content of phytoncides, lemon peel is a natural antibiotic that will help with flu, colds, sore throats. Therefore, in the autumn-winter period, especially during epidemics, it is advisable to add it to drinks for preventive purposes.

It is recommended to introduce lemon peel into your diet for diabetics. It not only inhibits the absorption of glucose, preventing a sharp increase in sugar, but also provides the body with all the substances it needs.

Lemon zest: benefits and harms for the human body

Despite the properties of lemon peel, which provide undoubted benefits, the peel is capable of causing harm to health.

Therefore, contraindications should be taken into account, which include:

  • - individual intolerance;
  • - ulcer;
  • - gastritis;
  • - severe sore throat (possible burns to the larynx);
  • - increased acidity of the stomach (otherwise heartburn will occur);
  • - chronic inflammation of the small intestine.
