5 TIPS FOR A HEALTHY LIFESTYLE

A healthy lifestyle is one that helps maintain and improve your health and wellness. There are many different things you can do to lead a healthy lifestyle, such as eating healthy, being physically active, keeping your weight healthy, and dealing with your stress. A healthy lifestyle is not just about healthy eating and exercise, but also about taking care of the 'whole you' - your physical, mental, emotional and spiritual well-being. And that means taking care of you from within.

LIVING A HEALTHY LIFESTYLE

While there are many common ways to live a healthy lifestyle, it actually looks different to everyone and means something from person to person. Whatever you choose, a healthy lifestyle is an important part of disease prevention, wellness and longevity. When you take into account your diet, physical activity and stress levels, you can effectively balance all aspects of your life - the "whole you." Below are 10 important things you can do to live a healthy lifestyle:

1. EAT A BALANCED DIET

A balanced diet doesn't have to be a boring diet. As long as you're getting enough protein, carbohydrates, vitamins, minerals, and fats, that piece of chocolate cake you fancy shouldn't be doing you too much harm. Enjoy your meals, chew your food properly, and eat a variety of foods, including five servings of fruits or vegetables a day. Too many processed foods or sugary foods can make you feel sluggish.

Drink more water. Most of us don't drink enough water every day. Water is essential for our body to function. Did you know that more than 60% of our body consists of water? Water is needed to perform bodily functions, to remove waste and to transport nutrients and oxygen throughout our body. Since we lose water every day through urine, bowel movements, perspiration and breathing, we need to replenish our water intake. Since food intake contributes about 20% to our fluid intake, which means we need to drink about 8-10 glasses a day to stay hydrated. One way to tell if you are hydrated - your urine should be colorless or pale yellow. If not, you are not getting enough water! Other signs are: dry lips, dry mouth, and little urination.

Eat more fruits and vegetables. Fruit contains many vitamins and minerals. As much as possible you should consume your vitamins and minerals through your daily diet. Satisfy your taste buds with these nutritious fruits: watermelon, apricots, avocado (yes, avocado is technically a fruit!), Apple, cantaloupe, grapefruit, kiwi, guava, papaya, strawberries. Like fruits, vegetables are important for good health. Experts suggest 5-9 servings of fruits / vegetables per day, but unfortunately it can be difficult at times. If possible, eat foods such as kidney beans, black beans, asparagus, long beans, green beans, and carrots. Think about your favorite vegetables and how to include more of them in your diet every day, and choose brightly colored foods. Fruits and vegetables with bright colors are good for health as they remove things in our body that damage our cells. So, you get fill of fruits / vegetables of different colors: White (Bananas, Mushroom), Yellow (Pineapple, Mango), Orange (Orange, Papaya), Red (Apple, Strawberries, Tomatoes, Watermelon), Green (Guava, Avocados) , Cucumber, Lettuce, Celery), Purple / Blue (blackberries, aubergine, plums).

2. TAKE TIME TO RELAX

Relaxation is very important for a healthy mind. However you relax - deep breathing, yoga, fishing, taking a bath, reading a book - try to chill for an hour a day if you can. Mental exhaustion can cause stress, depression and anxiety disorders. Modern lifestyles can be hectic - when you're not working, you're running after the kids or racing through the supermarket or doing something else. Relax! Take a deep breath. Even fifteen minutes of this is better than none, but try for an hour a day.

Deliberately breathe in deeply. Oxygen is a vital source of life. You may know how to breathe, but are you breathing properly? Most of us don't breathe properly - we only breathe superficially, breathing to 1/3 of our lung capacity. A full breath is one where your lungs are completely filled, your abdomen expands, and there is minimal movement in your shoulders. There are many benefits of deep breathing, including a reduction in stress and blood pressure, strengthening of abdominal and intestinal muscles, and relief from general body aches. Deep breathing also aids in better blood flow, releasing toxins from the body and helping you get a better night's sleep.

3. GET STARTED!

There is no excuse not to exercise as there is literally something for everyone. Not everyone likes to hit the treadmill or pump iron at the gym, but anything that gets your heart rate up is good cardiovascular activity. How about playing tennis, meeting a friend for a swimming session or self defense or martial arts? If you can't think of anything better, run up and down the stairs a few times. Feeling Better Now? Exercise releases endorphins, which cheer you up.

Meditate often. Meditation calms your mind and calms your soul. It can also help you deal with and cope with the stress in your life.

Exercise regularly. If you can exercise, don't do it a few times a week, but every day. Exercise is the key to a healthy life. Exercising daily can improve your health in many ways. It can help extend your life, lower your risk of disease, help you develop higher bone density and lose weight. One simple thing you can do, especially for short distances, is to walk, drive, or transport. You can climb the stairs instead of taking the elevator. You can choose exercises that you can easily do at home or outside and that you enjoy. When you enjoy the physical activities of your choosing, you will likely enjoy them and naturally want to do them. Exercise is about being healthy and having fun at the same time. Mixing up your exercises will keep them interesting.

4. REDUCE SMOKING AND DRINKING

Have you ever met a smoker who is glad they started smoking? Me neither. It's the most destructive thing you can do to your body. Drinking can be harmful if you drink or drink too much alcohol, but, like most things, moderation is key. In fact, a glass of red wine has proven to be good for the heart. But too much can be devastating to your health. Smoking can significantly increase the risk of lung cancer, kidney cancer, esophageal cancer, heart attack, and more. Smoking "light" cigarettes also does not reduce health risks. If you smoke, quit now and do it not only for yourself, but for your family and friends as well. Secondhand smoking (breathing smokers' air) can cause many of the same long-term illnesses as direct smoking. There is no risk-free level of secondhand smoke; even brief exposure can be harmful to your health. If possible, stay away from smokers and avoid cigarette smoke whenever possible.

5. REDUCE OILY AND SUGARY FOOD, SOFT DRINKS AND CAFEINE

If possible, reduce your intake of fast foods, French fries, donuts, chips, wedges, and fried foods. Not only are they very fattening (1 tablespoon of oil is 120 calories), fried foods contain acrylamide, a potential cancer-causing chemical. There are better alternatives, such as grilled, steamed, stir-fried or even raw foods. Eating less sugar is also better for your health. These are things like candy bars, pastries, chocolates, cookies, cakes and jelly donuts. Not only do they not fill you, they make you want to eat more because of the sugar rush. Instead, opt for healthy snacks such as fruits, salads, pure juices, and whole foods. Beverages with caffeine are diuretics, which means that they accelerate the rate of urine production. This means that they don't hydrate you as well as regular water can. Soda is also unhealthy, causes weight gain, and is an artificial stimulant. Instead, replace your soda with water or vegetable juices.
